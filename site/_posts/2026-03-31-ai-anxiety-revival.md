## TL;DR
Gen Z is experiencing AI anxiety, driving them to revisit old tech like CDs, DVDs, and Nintendo DS games for nostalgia and connection. As a result, the prices of these obsolete technologies are rising. ## What's Happening
The rise of AI anxiety among Gen Z has led to a surprising resurgence of old tech, including CDs, DVDs, and Nintendo DS games. This trend is not limited to gaming, as young people are also seeking out old computers, phones, and other obsolete technologies. According to a recent report, the prices of these old tech items have increased significantly, with some rare Nintendo DS games selling for hundreds of dollars. The report also notes that many UK shops are now dedicating entire sections to retro gaming and old tech, catering to the growing demand from Gen Z. For instance, a store in London's Camden Market is selling original Nintendo DS consoles for £200, while a rare copy of "Pokémon Diamond" can cost up to £500.

The nostalgia factor is a significant driver of this trend, as many young people are seeking to relive their childhood memories or experience the technologies that their parents or grandparents used. However, there's also a sense of community and connection that comes with collecting and playing old games, which is missing from modern online gaming experiences. As one collector noted, "There's something special about holding a physical copy of a game, and the thrill of the hunt for rare titles is a big part of the fun." The rise of social media platforms dedicated to retro gaming and old tech has also fueled this trend, providing a space for enthusiasts to share their collections, trade games, and connect with like-minded individuals.

The trend is not limited to the UK, as similar stores and communities are emerging in other countries, including the US, Japan, and Australia. The global demand for old tech and retro gaming is driving innovation, with companies like Nintendo and Sony releasing retro consoles and games to cater to this growing market. For example, Nintendo's NES Classic Edition, released in 2016, was a huge success, selling over 4 million units worldwide. The success of these retro consoles has also led to the development of new retro-style games, such as "Shovel Knight" and "Axiom Verge," which have become cult classics among retro gaming enthusiasts.

## Deep Dive
From a technical perspective, the resurgence of old tech is an interesting phenomenon, as it highlights the limitations and drawbacks of modern technologies. Many modern games, for example, rely heavily on online connectivity and cloud computing, which can be problematic for players with slow internet speeds or those who prefer to play offline. In contrast, old games and consoles offer a more straightforward, plug-and-play experience that is free from the complexities and frustrations of modern gaming. Additionally, the tactile experience of holding a physical game cartridge or disc, and the satisfaction of completing a difficult level or achieving a high score, are aspects of gaming that are often missing from modern digital experiences.

The market data also suggests that the demand for old tech is not limited to gaming, as sales of vintage computers, phones, and other obsolete technologies are also on the rise. This trend is driven in part by the growing interest in retro computing and the desire to experience the technologies of the past. However, it's also a reflection of the growing dissatisfaction with modern technologies, which are often seen as overly complex, expensive, and environmentally unsustainable. As one expert noted, "The resurgence of old tech is a sign of a broader cultural shift, as people begin to question the value and impact of modern technologies on their lives and the environment."

The rise of AI anxiety is also a significant factor in this trend, as many young people are seeking to disconnect from the digital world and find comfort in more tangible, human experiences. The constant stream of information and the pressure to be constantly connected can be overwhelming, and old tech offers a welcome respite from the stresses of modern life. As one collector noted, "Playing old games or listening to music on a physical format is a way for me to unplug and relax, and it's something that I find really therapeutic."
